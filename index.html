MA 4% Surtax on High Income
Risk
  • What: Additional 4% tax on all taxable income (including capital gains) above $1,083,150 for 2025 (indexed for inflation annually).
  • Impact on you: Profitable flip sales or property dispositions that push your income above the threshold trigger 9% effective state capital gains rate (up from 5%).
  • Strategy: Time property sales across tax years to stay under the threshold. Use 1031 exchanges to defer gains. Consider installment sales to spread recognition over multiple years.
Business Impact
100% Bonus Depreciation — Permanent
Opportunity
  • What: The One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA) permanently restored 100% first-year bonus depreciation for qualified property acquired after Jan 19, 2025.
  • Impact on you: All personal property in rental units (appliances, HVAC, flooring) and qualified improvement property (interior renovations to commercial spaces) can be fully expensed in year one.
  • Strategy: Perform cost segregation studies on every property to reclassify building components (e.g., lighting, landscaping, parking lots) from 39-year to 5/7/15-year life, then apply 100% bonus depreciation. This can generate massive paper losses to offset rental income and flip gains.
Business Impact
MA $1M+ Transfer Withholding
Action Required
  • What: As of Nov 1, 2025, all real estate transfers with gross sales price ≥ $1M require 4% withholding at closing (non-residents). Full-year MA residents are exempt.
  • Impact on you: As a MA resident you're exempt from withholding, but your hard money lending borrowers who are non-residents will be affected. Flip buyers from out of state will have reduced proceeds at closing.
  • Strategy: Ensure your borrowers (hard money clients) are aware of this rule. For your own sales, confirm residency documentation is on file. For fix-and-flip deals, factor the withholding impact into deal analysis for non-resident partners.
Business Impact
QBI Deduction — Now Permanent
Opportunity
  • What: The 20% Section 199A qualified business income deduction has been made permanent by OBBBA. Was set to expire end of 2025.
  • Impact on you: Rental income, carwash income, and lending income all potentially qualify for a 20% deduction on pass-through income, reducing your effective federal rate.
  • Strategy: Ensure each business entity is structured to maximize QBI. If any business is a specified service trade or business (SSTB), income limits apply — consider restructuring. Your rental activities should qualify under the safe harbor (250+ hours) or real estate professional status.
Business Impact
Qualified Opportunity Zones — Permanent
Opportunity
  • What: OBBBA made QOZ provisions permanent and requires new zone designations by July 1, 2026, effective Jan 1, 2027. Deferral and basis adjustment rules extended.
  • Impact on you: Capital gains from property sales or other investments can be deferred by reinvesting in QOZ funds. New zones will open up fresh investment territory in Massachusetts.
  • Strategy: Identify current and upcoming MA Opportunity Zones. When selling flip properties, roll gains into QOZ real estate investments. This can pair with your hard money lending — fund projects in opportunity zones for additional tax benefits.
Business Impact
MA Housing & Conversion Tax Credits
New for 2025
  • What: Two new nonrefundable credits: (1) for developers building new housing units (amount set by MA Housing Finance Agency), and (2) for converting commercial property to residential/mixed-use (up to 10% of conversion costs, set by EOHLC).
  • Impact on you: If you convert any commercial holdings to residential or develop new units, you may qualify for meaningful state tax credits.
  • Strategy: Evaluate underperforming commercial properties for residential conversion potential. The 10% credit on conversion costs plus federal bonus depreciation on improvements creates a powerful tax incentive stack.
Business Impact
1031 Exchange — Still Your Best Friend
Ongoing
  • What: 1031 like-kind exchanges continue to defer both federaland Massachusetts capital gains taxes. No changes to the program under OBBBA.
  • Impact on you: When selling rental properties, you can defer the full federal + MA tax burden (up to 9% state + 20% federal + 3.8% NIIT).
  • Strategy: For rentals you're ready to exit, use 1031 exchanges to trade into higher-quality or Opportunity Zone properties. Combine with cost segregation on the replacement property to generate immediate depreciation deductions.
Business Impact
Estate Tax Exemption — $15M Permanent
Opportunity
  • What: OBBBA made the doubled federal estate/gift exemption permanent at $15M per person ($30M joint) for 2026, indexed for inflation. Note: MA estate tax exemption remains at $2M (much lower).
  • Impact on you: Federal estate planning pressure eased, but Massachusetts still taxes estates above $2M — this is a significant gap for real estate portfolios.
  • Strategy: Use irrevocable trusts, family LLCs, and valuation discounts to keep your MA taxable estate under $2M. Gift properties or LLC interests during your lifetime to reduce estate value. The $15M federal exemption gives ample room for gifting strategies.

100% Bonus Depreciation on Equipment
Major Win
  • What: All carwash equipment purchased and placed in service after Jan 19, 2025 qualifies for 100% first-year expensing — tunnel systems, water reclamation, dryers, vacuums, POS systems, and more.
  • Impact: Upgrading or expanding your carwash operation now carries an enormous tax advantage. Full cost written off in year one.
  • Strategy: Accelerate any planned equipment upgrades or expansion to take full advantage. Purchase new tunnel equipment, water recycling systems, or automated payment kiosks — write off 100% immediately. Pair with Section 179 for any costs exceeding bonus depreciation eligibility.
Business Impact
Section 179 Expensing — $2.56M for 2026
Opportunity
  • What: Section 179 now allows up to $2.56M in immediate deductions for 2026 (up from previous limits), with phase-out at $4.09M. Covers equipment, vehicles, and certain improvements.
  • Impact: Your carwash vehicles (detail vans, supply trucks over 6,000 lbs GVWR) can qualify for accelerated deductions. Heavy vehicles over 14,000 lbs get full Section 179 without luxury caps.
  • Strategy: Coordinate equipment and vehicle purchases across your businesses. Stack Section 179 first, then apply bonus depreciation on remaining basis. Document business use carefully (>50% required).
Business Impact
MA Hazardous Waste & Environmental Compliance
Compliance
  • What: Massachusetts received final EPA authorization for updated RCRA hazardous waste program revisions (effective Nov 4, 2025). MassDEP continues to regulate wastewater discharge and chemical handling.
  • Impact: Carwash operations must comply with updated wastewater discharge permits, chemical storage regulations, and waste disposal rules.
  • Strategy: Audit your current wastewater treatment and chemical management systems. Invest in water reclamation (qualifies for bonus depreciation) to reduce discharge and lower compliance risk. Keep documentation current for MassDEP inspections.
Business Impact
SALT Deduction Increase
Tax Savings
  • What: SALT deduction cap raised from $10K to $40,400 for 2026 (with 1% annual increases through 2029).
  • Impact: Your Massachusetts property taxes and state income taxes on carwash profits are now more deductible on your federal return.
  • Strategy: Re-evaluate whether itemizing now makes more sense. The $40K cap can offset significant MA state taxes, especially with the 4% surtax hitting higher earners. Work with your CPA to model the full benefit.

Business Interest Deduction Expansion (Sec 163j)
Opportunity
  • What: OBBBA now computes adjusted taxable income (ATI) without subtracting depreciation, amortization, and depletion — effectively increasing the amount of business interest you can deduct.
  • Impact: If your carwash has equipment loans or an acquisition loan, more interest expense is now deductible.
  • Strategy: Review financing on carwash equipment and real estate. The expanded interest deduction may make debt-financed expansion more tax-efficient. Consider whether refinancing at current terms makes sense given the expanded deductibility.

MA Division of Banks — Licensing Amendments
Action Required
  • What: On Sept 23, 2025, the MA Division of Banks finalized amendments to the Mortgage Lender and Broker Licensing Law, Small Loans Law, and Truth in Lending Act. Changes update licensing procedures, disclosure rules, and financial-responsibility standards.
  • Impact: If you make 5+ residential mortgage loans in a 12-month period, you need a license. The Division now reviews profitability, historical net losses, and other prudential indicators when evaluating financial responsibility.
  • Strategy: Audit your loan volume. If approaching the 5-loan residential threshold, either obtain a license or focus lending on commercial/business-purpose loans (which are generally exempt). Update your disclosures and compliance documentation to match the new rules.
Business Impact
Business-Purpose Lending Exemption
Strategy
  • What: Loans made solely for business purposes (e.g., to fund fix-and-flip projects or commercial rehabs) are generally exempt from MA mortgage licensing requirements and many federal consumer protection rules (TILA, RESPA).
  • Impact: Structuring your hard money loans as business-purpose loans significantly reduces regulatory burden.
  • Strategy: Ensure all loan documentation clearly states the business purpose. Require borrowers to sign business-purpose certifications. Never lend for owner-occupied residential purchases without proper licensing. This is your biggest compliance lever.
Business Impact
MA Usury Laws — 20% Criminal Threshold
Compliance
  • What: Massachusetts prescribes 6% legal interest unless agreed otherwise in writing. Charging above 20% per annum triggers criminal usury penalties (up to 10 years imprisonment, $10K fine).
  • Impact: With market rates at 9–14%, plus points and fees, total effective rates can approach limits. Points, origination fees, and other charges may be counted toward the usury calculation.
  • Strategy: Calculate your all-in effective rate including points and fees on every loan to ensure you stay below 20%. Have your attorney review fee structures. For higher-risk loans, use equity participation or profit-sharing instead of higher interest rates.

Growth

  • Impact: Borrowers will increasingly seek hard money loans for QOZ projects to meet the substantial improvement requirements within 30 months.
  • Strategy: Position your lending business to serve QOZ investors. Market your speed advantage — QOZ investors need fast capital deployment. Consider creating QOZ-specific loan products with terms aligned to the 30-month improvement timeline.

MA $1M+ Withholding — Borrower Impact
Awareness
  • What: Your non-resident borrowers selling properties ≥$1M will have 4% withheld at closing. This reduces their available cash for loan repayment.
  • Impact: Non-resident borrowers on high-value flips may struggle to repay your loan at closing if they haven't budgeted for the withholding.
  • Strategy: Add withholding awareness to your underwriting process. For non-resident borrowers on $1M+ deals, ensure the exit analysis accounts for the 4% withholding. Consider requiring a withholding reserve or adjusting LTV accordingly.
Business Impact
Lead Paint Compliance for Funded Projects
Risk
  • What: Massachusetts has some of the strictest lead paint laws in the nation (Ch. 111 § 197). Properties built before 1978 require deleading when a child under 6 resides there. Deleading must be performed by licensed contractors.
  • Impact: Many fix-and-flip loans fund renovations on pre-1978 properties. Borrowers who skip lead abatement face massive liability — which is also a risk to your collateral value.
  • Strategy: Require lead inspection reports in your underwriting for pre-1978 properties. Include deleading costs in the renovation budget and loan amount. This protects your collateral and helps borrowers avoid legal issues.
